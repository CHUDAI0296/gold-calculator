Precious Metals Tips

Sterling Silver

Sterling silver is an alloy containing 92.5% silver and 7.5% other metals, usually copper.

  • Look for "925" or "Sterling" markings
  • Sterling silver has a bright white luster
  • It's commonly used in jewelry and tableware
Platinum

Platinum is a dense, malleable, and valuable precious metal:

  • Typically 95% pure in jewelry (950 platinum)
  • Heavier than gold of the same volume
  • Naturally white metal that doesn't tarnish
  • Look for "Pt", "950", or "Plat" markings
Market Fluctuations

Precious metal prices can fluctuate significantly:

  • Silver is typically more volatile than gold
  • Platinum historically trades at a premium to gold
  • Economic uncertainty often increases precious metal prices
  • Industrial demand affects silver and platinum prices
